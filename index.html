About France Language

The foundational structure of the French language was established when indigenous Celtic
populations assimilated the Vulgar Latin introduced during the Roman conquest. Subsequent
Frankish conquests during the early medieval period significantly influenced the phonetic
development and lexical expansion of this nascent Romance language. Throughout the Middle
Ages, the specific langue d'oïl dialect spoken in the vicinity of Paris achieved linguistic
dominance due to the centralization of royal authority.

This status was permanently cemented by the Ordinance of Villers-Cotterêts in 1539, which
formally instituted French as the exclusive language of jurisprudence and state administration,
paving the way for the establishment of the Académie Française in 1635 to meticulously codify
the orthographic and grammatical standards that continue to govern the language today.
